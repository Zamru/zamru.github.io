ABOUT
 
Zamru is an independent Jewish community in Princeton organized around monthly Shabbat services and dinners.

Zamru is dedicated to transformative, engaging, joyful and heartfelt prayer. Nearly the entire Kabbalat Shabbat service is sung out loud, to tunes some of which are familiar and some of which may be new. Zamru is privileged to feature Dan Nadel, a soulful musician who elevates our prayers. Our music is a fusion of styles including traditional nusach, Carlebach-style niggunim (wordless tunes), and Sephardic melodies. Services are always followed by a delicious vegetarian Shabbat meal.

Zamru is driven by a commitment to inclusive prayer – where each of us, regardless of our background – could participate, learn something new, and ask ourselves, what does this mean? We come together to pray for so many varying reasons: to cultivate gratitude, to talk to God, to be a part of a community, to experience joy, to awaken compassion, to celebrate Shabbat, to orient ourselves, to remind ourselves that the world is in need of healing, and to give thanks.
Leadership
 
Zamru is cross-denominational and is not affiliated with any of the Jewish movements. Zamru is led entirely by volunteers: Rabbi Julie Roth (Executive Director of the Center for Jewish Life - Princeton Hillel), Margaret Berger, Dean Edelman, Pam Edelman, Debbie Freedman, Melissa Lane, Avi Paradise, and Brenda Bodenheimer Zlatin.
Musical Director
 
A guitarist and composer, Dan Nadel’s search for his own sound has taken him on a journey across the globe, from Israel to NY, Brazil, and Spain. With a style that combines jazz, flamenco, and other influences, he performs frequently in both Jazz and Jewish venues throughout New York and around the world. Nadelmusic.com
Our Name
 
Zamru is a Hebrew word that means Sing! In Psalm 98:5, part of the Kabbalat Shabbat service, the psalmist proclaims, zamru ladonai b'kinor … Sing praise to God with the lyre – with the lyre and melodious song. The word zamru captures this community's desire to pray to God with voice and instruments and beautiful melodies.
Families
 
At Zamru, Shabbat is a joyful occasion to be celebrated by the entire family. To that end, children are welcome at our services. We invite their involvement in every aspect of the service -- a child's enthusiastic participation will only add to the vibrancy of the shared prayer experience. Our dinners are also family friendly.

DONATE
 
Zamru is a tax-exempt organization. We have no formal membership structure, and everyone is welcome to participate. However, we do have an annual budget of approximately $8,000 which covers space rental, music and supplies for services and potlucks.

Zamru is financed exclusively by contributions from individuals who participate in and enjoy our community. The money you donate will be used to fund operating and programming costs for Zamru. Any size donation is greatly appreciated. Sponsorship options are also available if you wish to honor an individual or commemorate a special occasion. If you are interested in sponsoring a service, please contact us at info@zamru.org.
